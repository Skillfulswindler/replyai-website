Features

Everything you need to handle reviews properly

Human-sounding replies

Every reply varies in sentence rhythm and word choice so nothing reads like it came off a production line. BrightLocal's blind study found that when people couldn't tell the difference between AI and owner-written replies, they actually preferred the AI replies.

Review age alerts

Every review gets a colour-coded age badge: green for under 2 days, amber for 3 to 6, red for 7 and over. 52% of customers expect a reply within a week, so you'll always know which ones are running out of time.

Google Posts writer

Write a short brief, pick a post type (What's New, Event, Offer, or Product), and get a finished Google Business post in seconds. It uses the same voice settings as your review replies.

Brand voice training

Paste a reply you're proud of or describe how you write in plain language. Every reply from then on is written to match your voice, not a generic AI default.

26 business types

Reply AI has 26 built-in business contexts, from dental clinics and law firms to restaurants, auto shops, and moving companies. Each one has its own prompt guidance, so the replies make sense for that specific type of business.

1-star protection

1-star and 2-star replies are always flagged for review before anything goes live. A poorly worded public response to a complaint can do more damage than the original complaint, so these ones you should always read before posting.

Common questions

Common questions

Will anyone know it was AI?
Probably not, and there's evidence to back that up. BrightLocal ran a blind study where consumers rated AI-generated replies and owner-written ones side by side. They couldn't tell the difference, and when they couldn't tell, they actually preferred the AI replies. Reply AI deliberately varies sentence rhythm and word choice so nothing sounds templated.
Does it work on all Google review pages?
Yes, it works on Google Search (when you click Reply on your Business Profile from Search), Google Maps, and Google Business Profile (business.google.com). The extension detects which page you're on and adds the reply widget automatically.
Does it actually post the reply for me?
Almost. Reply AI fills in Google's reply text box with the generated reply. You still click Post Reply yourself, which is intentional: you should read what's going up on your public profile before it does, especially on negative reviews. One more click and it's live, but you're always in control.
What happens with 1-star reviews?
1-star and 2-star replies always get flagged for review before anything goes live. The prompt for low-star reviews is written differently: it acknowledges the specific complaint, owns what went wrong, moves anything sensitive to a private channel, and stays composed regardless of how harsh the review was. These are the replies that future customers read most carefully, so they're treated accordingly.
Can I edit the reply before posting?
Yes, the generated reply appears in an editable card before it goes anywhere. You can edit it directly, hit "Different reply" to generate a new one, or copy it to the clipboard. Nothing goes into the Google textarea until you click "Post reply."
What are the 26 business types?
Restaurant/cafe, dental/medical clinic, HVAC/home services, retail store, hotel/hospitality, real estate agency, law firm, gym/fitness studio, auto repair shop, hair/beauty salon, accounting/finance, veterinary clinic, cleaning service, landscaping/lawn care, childcare/daycare, photography/videography, pet grooming, moving company, plumbing/electrical, spa/wellness centre, tutoring/education, chiropractor/physiotherapy, insurance agency, coffee shop/specialty cafe, e-commerce/online store, and general local business as the catch-all. Each has its own prompt guidance. A dental reply never reads like an auto shop reply.
Is my review data private?
Review text is sent to our backend (hosted on Railway) and to Anthropic's API to generate the reply. It is not stored permanently, sold, or used for training. Your settings (business name, tone, brand voice) are stored locally in your browser using Chrome's storage API. They never leave your device except as part of the generate request.
